02

Three Trajectories

The Wasted Effort

951,111 false alarms in 17 years. 75.7% were automatic fire alarms going off by mistake. Nearly 1 in 2 calls is a wasted trip.

The Decline

Fire incidents dropped 50% since 2009. Building regulations and smoke alarms are working. Dwelling fires: 27.8%, outdoor: 26.3%.

The Rise

Special service share grew from 31% to 41%. Forced entries +86%. Agency assists +433%. The fire brigade is becoming a multi-purpose emergency service.

Fires are falling. But who is still burning?

03

Who Still Burns?

The Seasonal Surprise

Most people assume winter is fire season. They're wrong. July has the most fires — outdoor fires surge 3.8× in summer while dwelling fires stay flat.

Signals of a Changing City

Three Signals

Each special service sub-type maps to a specific urban pressure. Scroll to explore them across London.

Forced Entry +86%

144,144 in 17 years. Stable until 2020, then surged post-COVID — ageing alone, locked out, welfare checks.

Flooding +10.6%

124,440 incidents. Modest growth but extreme volatility — driven by single weather events along the Thames.

Agency Assist +433%

From 1,019 to 5,427. Explosive growth — fire services supporting police, NHS, councils.

The Inner-Outer Divide +28.6% outer

Inner London SS growth: +3.5%. Outer London: +28.6%.

New demand is shifting outward — precisely where response times are already 20% slower.

05

Reading the City

London Fire Brigade answers over 100,000 calls a year.
Fewer than 1 in 10 are actual fires.

The rest — floods, forced entries, agency assists — are signals of a city ageing, warming, and stretching its safety nets.

Urban Analytics lets us read these signals and ask: is the system resilient enough?
